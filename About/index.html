Who am I?

I've spent a lot of energy trying to answer this question. It's part of the reason I studied physics, and partly what keeps me curious about the world. I believe the more I understand the world, the more I understand my context in it, and ultimately the more I learn about myself. I am not my education, my projects or my experiences, those things are just symptoms of what I am: curious.

Curiosity has always been my number one motivator. It is part of what makes me who I am. Naturally, this means that whenever I have nothing else to do, I find myself learning something new. After graduating high school, I didnt know what I wanted to do, so I took a gap year. During that gap year I started teaching myself physics and fell in love with physics. Now, having just finished my physics degree, I am in a similar situation. I don't know what my future holds, yet I have found myself again learning and falling in love with programming and data science. Learning is part of who I am and even if I wanted to I don't think I could ever stop.

Always Learning.

Get Out of the Comfort Zone.

The comfort zone is my worst enemy, as cheesy as that sounds. I went through something traumatic in high school that made me retract into myself. I noticed that the more I neglected to do difficult things, the more things became difficult. I reached a point where even going to the convenience store was a scary prospect, despite not having a problem doing things like that just a few months before. It was a difficult cycle where the more I gave in to fear, the smaller my comfort zone became. I reached a point where I decided that I wasn't going to let this anxiety rule my life any more and made an effort to do more difficult things. I found that as I did more difficult things, the less difficult they became and I had reversed the cycle. Now I have no problem doing things I never would have thought myself capable of before, like running a marathon, or backpacking alone through Europe.

The future is uncertain. What with the pandemic, climate change, and the technological singularity looming in the not-so-distant future, the next few decades are going to be nothing like anyone has seen before. The one certainty is that technology and data are going to have an increasingly important role in our lives, for better or for worse. I want to be part of this industry not only because it fascinates me but because if I can take part in technological advancement, I might be in a position to help steer it towards the better instead of the worse.

Looking to the Future.
